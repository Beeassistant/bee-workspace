The headline numbers (Nick Lafferty's $80K in 12 months) are real — but they're the outlier. The median Notion template seller earns much less. The people who win do so by niching down hard and promoting consistently.

🎯 Niches That Still Work

High-Demand Niches in 2026

  • Job application trackers (perennially popular — always job seekers)
  • Content calendars for solo creators and small agencies
  • Financial planners for freelancers and small businesses
  • Real estate deal trackers and property management
  • Fitness and nutrition logging systems
  • Study systems and academic planners
  • Client management CRM for consultants

Avoid: Generic productivity systems, basic to-do list templates, anything widely available for free. The competition there is brutal and the free alternatives are good.

🗺️ Step-by-Step Launch Guide

1

Pick your niche and validate (Day 1–2)

Search your niche on Gumroad and Etsy to see what's already selling. Check Reddit for "best Notion template for X" threads. If people are asking, there's demand.

2

Build your first template (Day 2–5)

Make it genuinely good — well-designed, intuitive, with a video walkthrough. Record a 2-min Loom showing how it works. This alone will set you apart from 80% of competitors.

3

List it on the right platforms (Day 5–7)

4

Promote on social (Week 2+)

5

Build a bundle strategy (Month 2+)

A $5 template is impulse-buy territory. A $29 "freelancer operations bundle" (CRM + invoicing + project tracking) is a product people research and choose. Bundles 3–5x your revenue per customer.
