## 💼 LINKEDIN POST

**Narrative:** Most founders treat the launch like a binary event — "Go live and see what happens." That's how you fail.

Launching a SaaS in 2026 requires a strategic playbook, not luck.

I've been digging into what separates successful SaaS launches from the rest, and here's what I'm seeing:

**The winners do this:**

**1. Validate obsessively before building**
50-100 customer interviews. Not surveys — *conversations*. You're testing a hypothesis, not collecting vanity metrics. Use AI tools to analyze sentiment across reviews and feedback. This tells you if the market actually wants what you're building.

**2. Define your ICP like your survival depends on it**
Because it does. Your Ideal Customer Profile should be specific enough that you can walk into a coffee shop and spot them. Then build everything — product, pricing, marketing — around that one person.

**3. Start with a Concierge MVP**
Automate last. Prove the concept first by delivering manually. This buys you time to learn what customers *actually* need vs. what you *think* they need.

**4. Measure the right things**
Forget vanity metrics. Track NPS (> 50), retention (> 80%), and emotional attachment ("would be very disappointed if it disappeared"). These signal true product-market fit.

**5. Build community before you launch**
The days of "stealth mode until launch" are over. Get in communities, add value, build trust. Your first 50 customers should come from people who *already know* you and believe in your vision.

The SaaS market is $736B and growing. But it's not for everyone. It's for founders willing to validate, iterate, and obsess over product-market fit before chasing scale.

Are you building in public?
